Louis Pasteur

Here's a time line of Pasteur's life:

  • 1822 – Born in Dole, France.
  • 1857 – Developed the germ theory, showing that microorganisms cause fermentation and disease.
  • 1862 – Disproved spontaneous generation by showing that microbes come from other microbes.
  • 1865 – Identified the cause of a disease in silkworms, saving the silk industry.
  • 1865–1870 – Invented pasteurization, a method to kill bacteria in liquids like milk and wine.
  • 1879 – Created his first vaccine for chicken cholera, using weakened germs.
  • 1881 – Developed and tested a vaccine for anthrax on animals.
  • 1885 – Successfully used a rabies vaccine on a young boy, saving his life.
  • 1887 – Founded the Pasteur Institute in Paris for medical research.
  • 1895 – Died in Marnes-la-Coquette, France.

    • In summary, Louis Pasteur was a pioneer in microbiology whose discoveries—such as germ theory, pasteurization, and vaccines—revolutionized science and medicine. His work saved lives, improved public health, and continues to impact the world today.
